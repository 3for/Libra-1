\subsection{Zero-Knowledge Verifiable Polynomial Delegation Scheme}
\label{subsec::zkvpd}

Let $\mathbb{F}$ be a finite field, $\mathcal{F}$ be a family of $\ell$-variate polynomial over $\mathbb{F}$, and $d$ be a variable-degree parameter \babis{this ``variable-degree" comes up all the time and you have not defined it. can you please define it?}\yupeng{defined at the beginning of section 2}. A zero-knowledge verifiable polynomial delegation scheme (zkVPD) consists of the following algorithms: 
\begin{itemize}
    \item $(\pp, \vp) \leftarrow \KeyGen(1^\lambda, \ell, d)$,
    \item $\comm \leftarrow \Commit(f, r_f, \pp)$,
    \item $\{\accept,\reject\}\leftarrow\CheckComm(\comm, \vp)$,
    \item $(y,\pi)\leftarrow\Open(f,t,r_f,\pp)$,
    \item $\{\accept,\reject\}\leftarrow\Verify(\comm,t,y,\pi,\vp)$.
\end{itemize}     
A zkVPD scheme satisfies correctness, soundness and zero knowledge, which we formally define in Appendix~\ref{app:zkvpd}.
